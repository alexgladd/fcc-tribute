Tribute to Margaret Hamilton

A pioneer in Computer Science

Hamilton was at one time the lead flight software designer for the Apollo program

Timeline of Margaret Hamilton's life:

  • 1935: Born in Paoli, Indiana, USA
  • 1958: Graduated with a B.A. in Mathematics from Earlham College
  • 1959: Took an interim position at MIT developing weather prediction software
  • 1961: Began work on the SAGE Project at Lincoln Lab
  • 1963: Began work on the Apollo program at MIT for NASA
  • 1969: Thanks in part to Margaret's software, Apollo 11 lands safely on the Moon
  • 1976: Founded Higher Order Software to develop ideas about software error prevention and fault tolerance
  • 1986: Founded Hamilton Technologies, Inc., where she still serves as CEO today

Notable Awards:

  • 1986: Ada Lovelace Award for women in computing
  • 2003: NASA Exceptional Space Act Award for scientific and technical contributions to NASA
  • 2016: Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian honor in the U.S.
